Customer Journey
From Discovery to Confirmed Booking
How a podcaster moves through the system — from finding the studio to walking in the door.
1

Podcaster Discovers the Studio

Via website, social media, referral, or paid ads. Lands on the studio's online presence and decides to explore membership or book.

2

Signs Up on the Portal

Creates an account with name, email, and phone number. A GHL contact record is automatically created in the background.

3

Chooses Membership Tier

Selects Non-Member, Gold, or VIP. Payment processed via Stripe for paid tiers. GHL automatically applies the correct tag — member-non, member-gold, or member-vip — to the contact record.

4

Logs into Portal Dashboard

Portal reads the GHL contact tag and instantly determines their tier. Shows only their tier's booking page — no other tier's services or pricing is ever visible. A Non-Member cannot see Gold prices. A Gold member cannot see VIP prices.

5

Selects Service & Time Slot

The booking page shows 3 services at their tier's pricing: Audio Recording, Video Podcast Session, and Live Streaming. Member picks the service, then picks an available date and time.

6

GHL Checks Availability (Automatic)

Three checks happen simultaneously before a slot is shown: Is a concurrent booking slot (staff) available? Is a physical studio room free? Is required equipment available (for Video or Streaming)? All three must pass for the slot to appear.

7

Booking Confirmed

A studio room is automatically assigned to the booking. Confirmation email + SMS sent instantly to the podcaster. The booking appears in the client's GHL dashboard under Appointments, with the assigned studio clearly listed.

Availability Logic
How the System Prevents Double-Bookings
Three independent layers work together. All three must be available for a time slot to appear to the customer.
👥

3 Staff Slots (Concurrent Capacity)

Three placeholder "studio slots" represent the maximum number of simultaneous bookings. Since there are 3 studios, a maximum of 3 sessions can run at the same time — across all tiers and all service types combined.

Example: 3 bookings exist at 2pm (any mix of tiers/services). A 4th person tries to book 2pm → all 3 staff slots are claimed → 2pm is not shown as available to them.
🏠

3 Rooms (Studio A, B, C)

Each physical studio is registered as a Room in GHL. Each room holds exactly 1 booking at a time. When a booking is confirmed, GHL auto-assigns an available room and claims it — blocking it across all calendars and all tiers simultaneously.

Example: VIP member books Studio A at 3pm. Studio A is now blocked for Non-Member and Gold bookings at 3pm too. Studios B and C remain available at 3pm.
🎛️

2 Equipment Types (Shared Gear)

Teleprompter (qty: 2) is linked to Video Podcast and Live Streaming calendars. Streaming Encoder (qty: 1) is linked to Live Streaming only. Running out of a required equipment item blocks that service even if staff slots and rooms are free.

Example: 2 Video Podcast sessions booked at 4pm → both teleprompters claimed → a 3rd Video Podcast at 4pm is blocked even if a studio is free.
